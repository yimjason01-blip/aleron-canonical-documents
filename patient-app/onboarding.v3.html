A few agreements first.

Each one is short and written in plain language. Tap to read, then agree to each individually.

Genetic testing is a separate decision.

Genetic results are permanent and can carry implications for your family. So this decision stands on its own, and it is entirely yours to make.

What the panel looks at

Inherited cancer risk, cardiovascular genetics, and how your body processes common medications. Not a raw gene list. Your physician interprets everything in context.

How results are handled

Some findings are called pathogenic or likely pathogenic, which means the variant is known to raise risk. Any result that is serious, ambiguous, or needs clinical context is always held for a conversation with your physician first.

Your data stays private

We never share or sell your genetic information, or anything derived from it. Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest, and only your care team can access it, solely to deliver your care. Your employer will never see your genetic results or any information derived from them, and your benefits are not affected by whether you participate.

Your control

You may request deletion of your genetic data and your biological samples at any time, and we honor it.

About your panel+

Your first panel measures cardiovascular markers such as ApoB and Lp(a), metabolic markers such as fasting insulin and HbA1c, inflammation, kidney and liver function, and a hormone baseline. Each result arrives with a plain language explanation of what it measures and why your physician ordered it.

About your genetic results+

The genetics panel looks at inherited cancer risk, cardiovascular genetics, and pharmacogenomics. Most people receive reassuring results. Anything serious or ambiguous is always walked through with your physician first, per the preference you set.

Notification preferences+

You'll be notified when your kit ships, when your sample is received, and when your physician has reviewed your results. You can quiet any of these in settings.
