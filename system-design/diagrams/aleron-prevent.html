The Aleron CVD risk model

PREVENT base · five phenotype modifiers · 10- & 30-year risk

Two layers, kept distinct. Layer 1 is AHA PREVENT (2023), a validated equation for 10- and 30-year total CVD (atherosclerotic events plus heart failure) and ASCVD from age, sex, cholesterol, blood pressure, diabetes, smoking, BMI, kidney function (eGFR), and treatment. It dropped the race term of the old Pooled Cohort Equations, and every number here is computed live from its published coefficients (validated against the AHA calculator). Layer 2 adds the five Aleron phenotype modifiers PREVENT does not capture: lipoprotein(a), ApoB/LDL-C discordance, hs-CRP, HOMA-IR, and cardiorespiratory fitness, applied as a transparent multiplicative overlay on the base. The 10-year total-CVD number is the calibrated anchor; the 30-year is directional. Build a patient under Layer 1, then layer the modifiers on top. Illustrative use; not medical advice.

BMI moves only the heart-failure outputs — its coefficient is zero in the ASCVD and total-CVD equations (its atherosclerotic effect is treated as fully mediated through BP, glucose, and lipids). Statin is a confounded marker here (statin users in the data were higher-risk), so toggling it barely moves — or slightly raises — risk; it is not a treatment effect.

The four lab modifiers multiply under one shared 2.0× cap; cardiorespiratory fitness is applied last and separately, since it can move risk either way. ApoB/LDL-C fires only on discordance (ApoB >130 with LDL-C <130). Thresholds are first-pass, anchored to literature, not yet locally calibrated. Outcome

Each bar = the change in 10-year risk as that one factor moves across its full range (best→worst), everything else held at a representative patient (age 55, TC 200, HDL 50, SBP 130, eGFR 90, female). A fixed sensitivity ranking — which levers move risk most — that does not change with the sliders above. Flip the outcome and the profile genuinely changes, because PREVENT fits each separately: cholesterol drives atherosclerosis but has zero weight in heart failure, while BMI drives heart failure but has zero weight in atherosclerotic / total CVD. Age and kidney function drive all three. Confounded treatment markers (BP meds, statin) are excluded; swings are one-at-a-time and don't sum.

Coefficients: AHA PREVENT base model (Khan SS et al., Circulation 2024;149:430–449), sex-specific. Base model excludes the optional enhancements (HbA1c, urine albumin-creatinine ratio, social-deprivation index). Cholesterol shown in mg/dL; eGFR by CKD-EPI 2021. Valid for ages 30–79.

Validated three independent ways: reference vectors vs the AHA calculator (exact to 3 decimals); the live widget vs a reference engine (max diff 3e-16 across 480 values); and a from-scratch reimplementation off the primary R-package sources, which also cross-checked all 372 coefficients + 12 intercepts between two implementations with zero mismatch (max diff 2e-16).
