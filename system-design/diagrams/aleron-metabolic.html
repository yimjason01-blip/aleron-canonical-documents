The Aleron metabolic risk model

Two gates · QDiabetes base + five modifiers (sigmoid) · severity vector for established T2D

Two gates, because the question changes once diabetes is present. Gate 1 (no diabetes) predicts 10-year incident T2D from QDiabetes-2018 (Hippisley-Cox 2017, derived on 8.87M patients, C-statistic ~0.89/0.87). Unlike the CVD widget's live PREVENT, QDiabetes is used here as a calibrated anchor you set directly. On top sits the fully-computed overlay: five modifiers — insulin resistance, central adiposity & weight trajectory, hepatic risk (FIB-4), family history, and cardiorespiratory fitness — combined through a log-odds sigmoid (kept, unlike the flat CVD/CKD layer, because Gate-1 base risk can be high enough that a flat product would overshoot), with the non-CRF contribution capped at 3.0× and CRF applied last. Gate 2 (established T2D) emits no probability — complications are already owned by the CVD, CKD, cancer, and neuro models — only a structured severity vector: glycemic tier, hepatic tier, treatment-adequacy gaps, and trajectory. Illustrative use; not medical advice.

Gate 1 · the five Aleron modifierslog-odds sigmoid · non-CRF contribution capped at 3.0× · CRF last

Each modifier contributes its single applicable log-HR; the four non-CRF modifiers sum in log-odds capped at 3.0×, then CRF is applied last as a 0.65–1.4× multiplier. Insulin resistance uses HOMA-IR (fasting-insulin and TyG are fallbacks when HOMA is unavailable). Central adiposity prefers waist-to-height; weight gain and loss are mutually exclusive. Model behaviors to knowdesign choices that are deliberate, not bugs

The metabolic layer routes by clinical question and keeps the combination honest at high base risk:

  • Gate 2 has no probability. Once T2D is established, complications are owned by the CVD, CKD, cancer, and neuro models; a separate severity probability would double-count. Gate 2 emits an action state, not a number.
  • The sigmoid is kept for Gate 1 (unlike the flat CVD/CKD layer) because QDiabetes base risk can be high enough that a flat product would overshoot — log-odds compress the stack instead.
  • Coverage gates the output. A base-only run (no informative modifier) holds at the base and lists the highest-value missing tests, rather than applying a CRF-only discount that implies unsupported precision.
  • Weight gain and loss are mutually exclusive, and central adiposity prefers waist-to-height (travels across sex and ethnicity) over a single BMI term.
  • Shared inputs are not double-counting. Raw metabolic inputs feed CVD, CKD, cancer, and neuro independently because each predicts a different outcome; the action layer dedupes the interventions.
  • Genetics route out. MODY genes, the diabetes PRS, PNPLA3, and TM6SF2 are diagnostic/reclassification signals handled outside this score, not phenotype modifiers.

Base model: QDiabetes-2018 with fasting-glucose/HbA1c enhancement (Hippisley-Cox J, Coupland C, BMJ 2017; PMID 29158232). Modifier thresholds, weights, the 3.0× non-CRF contribution cap, the sigmoid combination, and the Gate-2 severity tiers per the Aleron Metabolic Phenotype Risk Model v3.0. Base anchors here are illustrative point estimates, not live recomputation; the Gate-1 modifier arithmetic is computed exactly as specified, including the 3.0× cap (which binds slightly earlier than the v3.0 M-002 prose, where the cap was not applied).
