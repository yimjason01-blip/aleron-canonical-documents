Genetic findings → must-do output · full Invitae panel

Prototype of Aleron's genetic must-do channel, expanded to the full proactive panel. How each monogenic finding is routed to a clinical mandate, a carrier note, a risk-factor flag — or no action.

A pathogenic / likely-pathogenic finding in an actionable gene is standard of care, so it does not enter the QALY-ranked map — it lands here. But "on the panel" is not "a mandate": only high/moderate-penetrance dominant findings become must-dos. Recessive single alleles become carrier notes; low-penetrance findings (heterozygous thrombophilia) become risk-factors, not standing orders. Magnitude rides along as justification, never as a ranking.

Source & scope. Roster anchored on Haverfield et al., BMC Medicine 2021 — the peer-reviewed study of this Invitae proactive panel — which enumerates 147 genes (matching the "~148" figure). The current commercial Comprehensive Genetic Risk Panel cites "up to 163 genes"; the ~16-gene delta added since 2020 is a flagged follow-on, not yet built (the catalog hides it behind JS/PDF). Decision-support scaffold, not a substitute for genetic counseling — every entry is gated on a confirmed result. Magnitude is justification, not a ranking. Routing is honest about strength: moderate-penetrance cancer genes are "consider surveillance," heterozygous thrombophilia is a situational risk-factor (never lifelong anticoagulation), recessive single alleles are carrier notes. Some entries are childhood-onset or beyond ACMG SF and marked as such. Penetrance bands, modes, and management were corrected by an adversarial verification pass (change log above).
