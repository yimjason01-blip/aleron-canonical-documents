The Aleron neurodegenerative risk model

CogDrisk-ML base · five phenotype modifiers · APOE4 as strategy, not score · 20-year all-cause dementia

Illustrative stratification prototype. CogDrisk-ML is not recomputed here and does not supply a validated 20-year absolute-risk endpoint. The base is entered manually. Modifier and APOE panels demonstrate proposed behavior only. No canonical Neuro runner or automated APOE surveillance pathway is active.

Two layers plus a gene that sits outside both. Layer 1 is CogDrisk-ML (Huque & Anstey 2025), a sex-specific midlife model for 20-year all-cause dementia derived on over 500K people (UK Biobank + ARIC). The source reports discrimination, not a published fixed-horizon calibrated risk, so — unlike the CVD widget's live PREVENT — the base is used here as a calibrated stratification anchor you set directly. Layer 2 is the fully-computed overlay: five modifiers — blood biomarkers (p-tau217, NfL), vascular/metabolic state, lifestyle/sensory factors, family history, and cardiorespiratory fitness — combined log-additively through a bounded sigmoid with per-domain caps, CRF applied last, and a global cap at 3.0× the base. The sigmoid is kept (unlike the flat CVD/CKD layer) because many modifiers can stack. APOE4 is deliberately not in the score: it is a consented strategy input that sets biomarker-surveillance cadence and prevention intensity. Illustrative use; not medical advice.

The base anchor is CogDrisk-ML's 20-year point estimate, entered here rather than recomputed (its full coefficients are not public in usable form). The 20-year horizon differs from CVD (10/30-yr) and Metabolic (10-yr); the modifier stack is not yet a validated integrated model on that horizon.

Layer 2 · the five Aleron modifierslog-additive sigmoid · per-domain caps · CRF last · global 3.0× cap

Each domain sums its applicable log-weights and is clamped to its own cap (biomarkers 3.0×, vascular 2.5×, lifestyle 2.5×, family 1.5×); the lifestyle domain uses partial credit (largest weight plus half the excess of the rest). CRF is applied last as a 0.60–1.4× multiplier and supersedes the physical-inactivity proxy when measured. When both biomarkers are elevated they combine with a 0.7 correlation-attenuation factor. Thresholds and weights are verbatim from the model spec.

Disclosed only with informed consent and counseling — the harm profile, not the predictive value, is the reason. APOE4 is non-modifiable, unlocks no asymptomatic drug, and carries real downside: distress, plus long-term-care, life, and disability insurance exposure that GINA does not protect. A negative is not reassuring in isolation: most dementia occurs in non-carriers. The raw genotype routes through the Result Router; what lives here is the consented strategy keyed to it.

What moves risk most · the five modifierssensitivity · full range of each domain at a fixed base · independent of the sliders

Each bar = the swing in adjusted 20-year risk as that one domain moves across its full range (best→worst), the others held at neutral and the base fixed at a representative 5%. This is a fixed sensitivity ranking — which domain can move risk most — and does not change with the sliders above. Biomarkers carry the widest range, which is the model's preclinical edge. CRF's range (0.6→1.4×) is the only lever that can also lower risk. One-at-a-time, so they don't sum.

How the combination behavesthis patient's modifier stack applied across the plausible base range

The modifiers are added in log-odds, not multiplied on the probability, so the adjusted curve compresses as the base rises — a stack that doubles a 3% base cannot double a 40% base. The faint dotted line is the identity (no modifiers); the solid line is the adjusted risk after CRF and the global 3.0× base cap. This sigmoid behavior is exactly why the neuro layer keeps log-odds rather than the flat product used for CVD and CKD.

Model behaviors to knowdesign choices that are deliberate, not bugs

The neuro layer is built to keep a non-modifiable gene from corrupting a phenotype score, and to keep stacking modifiers bounded:

  • APOE4 never moves the number. A non-modifiable gene multiplied onto CogDrisk would mis-frame direct genetic risk as a routine modifier, break the base calibration, and surface a number the patient may be harmed by and did not consent to learn. It sets who to watch and how often, and raises prevention intensity — the biomarkers answer when pathology starts.
  • The sigmoid and 3.0× cap are retained (unlike the flat CVD/CKD layer) precisely because many modifiers stack; they prevent a runaway product.
  • CRF supersedes the physical-inactivity proxy when fitness is measured — the better signal replaces the cruder one rather than double-counting.
  • Shared inputs are not double-counting. Hypertension and T2D feed both CVD and Neuro with different outcome-specific weights; the action layer dedupes the interventions.
  • Stroke belongs to CVD. The base captures chronic neurodegeneration including vascular dementia; the acute cerebrovascular event is CVD's domain, which prevents double-counting.
  • Monogenic variants route out. PSEN1/2, APP (early-onset AD) and LRRK2, GBA1, SNCA (PD) go to deterministic protocols, not the probabilistic score. An NfL-elevated, p-tau217-normal patient may be early synucleinopathy — surfaced as interpretation, not a model decision.
