The Aleron CKD risk model

Two-stage base anchor · five phenotype modifiers · 5-year kidney risk

Two layers, kept distinct. Layer 1 is the validated base: a two-stage router that sends each patient to the right published equation — CKD-PC incident-CKD (Nelson 2019, 5-year probability eGFR falls below 60, derived on 5.2M people) for patients with no established CKD, or the Kidney Failure Risk Equation (Tangri 2016, C-statistic 0.90, KDIGO-endorsed) once CKD is present. Unlike the CVD widget — whose PREVENT base is recomputed live from public coefficients — these equations are used here as a calibrated anchor you set directly; their coefficients are not reproduced. Layer 2 is the fully-computed part: the five Aleron phenotype modifiers the base equation does not capture — cystatin C discordance, UACR trajectory, blood-pressure control, glycemic/insulin resistance, and nephrotoxin exposure — multiplied under one shared 3.0× cap. Set a base anchor under Layer 1, then layer the modifiers on top. Illustrative use; not medical advice.

The base anchor is the published model's 5-year point estimate, entered here rather than recomputed. Routing follows the doc: eGFR below 60 or UACR at or above 30 mg/g moves a patient from Stage A (CKD-PC) to Stage B (KFRE, kidney-failure probability), where UACR is already a core base input.

Layer 2 · the five Aleron modifiersbase anchor × phenotype signals it doesn't capture · live on the Layer-1 patient

All five modifiers multiply under one shared 3.0× cap — there is no separately-applied lever here (unlike CVD's fitness term). Each modifier contributes its single most-applicable rung; nephrotoxins take the highest rung, they do not stack. Missing modifiers default to 1.0 and are flagged as low coverage, not read as reassurance. Thresholds and magnitudes are taken verbatim from the model spec; the base above is the validated anchor.

What moves risk most · the five modifierssensitivity · full range of each modifier at a fixed base · independent of the sliders

Each bar = the swing in adjusted 5-year risk as that one modifier moves across its full range (best→worst hazard ratio), the others held at neutral and the base fixed at a representative 8%. This is a fixed sensitivity ranking — which modifier can move risk most — and does not change with the sliders above. Blood-pressure control carries the widest range; glycemic shows its diabetic-range maximum (×1.5). One-at-a-time, so they don't sum.

How the combination behavesthis patient's modifier stack applied across the plausible base range

Adjusted risk = base × min(product, 3.0). The flat product is linear in the base, so the line is straight — but the 3.0× cap means a heavily-stacked phenotype cannot inflate a low base without bound. Dashed = the uncapped product for reference; where it separates from the solid line, the cap is binding. The flat layer is honest at these low base risks because the product and a sigmoid agree here (per the spec); the high-base models (neuro, metabolic) keep a sigmoid instead.

Model behaviors to knowdesign choices that are deliberate, not bugs

The modifier layer is built to avoid double-counting the base and to keep every number traceable:

  • Family history is not scored. Its association attenuates to non-significant after adjusting for eGFR and albuminuria, which are already base inputs. It tightens monitoring cadence and can route to renal-depth genetic testing — it does not move the number.
  • Resting heart rate is carried, not scored — a weak wearable marker of sympathetic overdrive, predictive but not a lever.
  • Persistent albuminuria routes, it doesn't multiply. UACR at or above 30 (or eGFR below 60) sends the patient to Stage B, whose KFRE base already takes UACR as a core input — scoring it again would double-count.
  • The base must use CKD-EPI 2021 creatinine-only eGFR so the cystatin C discordance modifier does not double-count filtration already in the base.
  • Low coverage is flagged, not imputed. A missing modifier defaults to 1.0 and surfaces as reduced coverage rather than implied reassurance.
