01 · Thesis, Hero & Position

Some of your best days are still ahead of you.

Thesis

The belief above is why the company exists. The thesis follows from it. Data for the days: intelligence assembled from your complete picture, led by a physician who knows you, in service of the moments you care about, never the reverse.

The hero

The hero is a high-agency person in midlife, roughly 40s to early 60s, who does not feel finished. They are quietly unwilling to let age, friction, or conventional healthcare define the next chapter. They run something: a company, a team, a practice, a household. They hire specialists, check the work before they trust it, and once they trust it they stop micromanaging. They are comfortable with data, read the footnotes when something matters, and notice when someone is bluffing.

They are not chasing vanity or biohacker theater. They want the physical capacity, clarity, and resilience to keep building, parenting, leading, loving, traveling, and becoming more themselves. Age has started to show up around them: parents getting older, a friend with a diagnosis, their own recovery taking longer than it used to. They assume medicine could do more for them than an annual physical does, but they do not know which provider to trust, and they do not have time to become an expert themselves.

They worry about losing independence later, and about wasting time and money now: wellness fads, tests that produce anxiety instead of decisions, services that talk down to them or upsell them. Health sits in the same category as their financial planning: important, handled carefully, not a hobby. In photographs they are magnetic, vital people around 45 to 60 who feel aspirational but plausibly you: lean, strong, beautifully aged, calm, and present. The register is quiet contentment, not joy: a held moment with a spouse, child, friend, craft, trail, workshop, ocean, or city view. The promise is not "look young forever." It is that your best work and best years may still be ahead of you.

Aleron identity

Aleron is navigation intelligence for the days ahead: the whole of medicine, read against the whole of you, distilled to one course correction at a time. You choose the destination; Aleron computes the course; your physician is in the pilot seat. The destination is not a figure of speech. A life ahead has a shape: capacity over time, with forks for the events that risk makes likelier and earlier. Seeing that shape, and choosing a better one, is the member's act. Everything behind it is the work of getting there: a multitude of clinical risk models, the patterns that cut across them, action candidates drawn from every relevant published study, scored and ranked down to one thing. One thing this month is a course correction. The work is enormous; the face is calm.

Three bodies, in fixed order. The member chooses where to go and stays the protagonist; the brand works one step off-frame, which is why the photography contains almost no doctors and no product. The physician is the one human in the loop: reads the instrument, makes the call, answers for it. Members describe that relationship as a friend who happens to be brilliant; the quote names the physician's voice, not the whole brand. The instrument computes and never steers: guidance, never autopilot. The name keeps the order honest: an aleron is an aileron, the surface on the wing where a decision becomes a change of course, and the hand on the stick is never the instrument's. This names the identity of Aleron itself: the instrument. The brand identity is what the whole document assembles, the interplay between that intelligence and the hero's destinations: the photographs hold the destination, the mono layer holds the course, and the wordmark seats the physician's credential beside the instrument's name.

Position

Every neighbor sells something close to this hero, and none sells this. The longevity clinics sell the future, surveilled: dread converted into control, denominated in biomarkers and scans. The lab apps sell your data, self-served. Executive medicine sells the year, cleared. Concierge primary care comes nearest and sells the present, served: the warmth of being known, with diagnostics as proof that the fee pays. The remaining position is ours: the future, served.

That phrase is the brief for this entire document. In this market the visual identity signals the position: surveillance wears dark-mode scans and electric accents, and the present wears cream and golden hour. Serving the future demands both registers in one system: warm at the surface, rigorous one layer down. Life, and the data that protects it, made one. The sections that follow build that system.

04 · Photography
Definition

A photograph works here when it transmits a sensation the viewer's body can feel: effort, cold, rain, weight, height, taste, with its cause visible in the frame. The subject is fully absorbed in their own world, never aware of the frame; emotion is a result, never a requirement. Different people, different pursuits, different continents, the same vivid state: each frame possibly one of the best days of someone's life.

The recipe · codified
SUBJECT
The only variable. The real object named, true to the item as it exists in the world today: which seams to ink, and where the one gold detail sits — always at the instrument's touch.
STYLE
Flat isometric illustration of a real, contemporary object — immediately recognizable, never a stylized prop. Drawn with the precision of a technical instruction drawing, never a 3D render: no photorealism, no soft shading, no gradients, no blur, no reflections, no texture. Fine uniform ink on the edges; ink only the seams the object truly has — no invented panel lines, large calm faces left clean. Each face exactly one flat cel value.
PALETTE
Paper FAF7F2 ground · cream F5F1E8 · indigo 252B3D · slate 5A6378 · ink 1D1B18 · exactly one small gold C9A14E detail on the object.
SHADOW
One hard-edged flat shape cast from the object's base, extending left, solid indigo 252B3D — never gray, never black. Light: a short slim sliver, far smaller than the object. Where indigo meets indigo, paper or the ink line keeps them distinct. Ratified for every depicted surface — a letter, a card, a screen shown as an object; live surfaces cast none.
FRAME
Isometric, centered, generous margins · no people, no text, no logos, no callout lines, no numbers.
ENGINE
gpt-image-2 · quality high · 1024 × 1024.

Six blocks; five fixed, rendered verbatim every time · subject is the only slot · the blocks live as constants in tools/gen-objects.py; objects render from the generator only
