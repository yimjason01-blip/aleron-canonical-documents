The chart is an instrument.

The physician product register, extracted from the per-patient chart build of 02 Jul 2026 the way the Cabin was decided from the register studies. The premise it stands on: the scan is the duty. The member reads; the physician scans, mid-clinic, then drills where the instrument points. Parse speed still decides every contest, but here it serves triage: find the abnormal, see what it changes, act. Materials, wordmark, interaction grid, and the gold and hazard laws are inherited from the brand deck and the design system unchanged. This document proposes the laws the chart forced; nothing below is ratified until it carries a date.

01 · The laws

Eight laws, extracted from the build.

  1. P1Flight-deck by default, register-clean always. The per-patient chart resolves through the flight-deck tokens; the design system frames the physician surface as the instrument and the inbox already sits in the dark. Day is the removal of one class, kept live for print and consult-room reading. If a state cannot be produced by the token swap alone, the component is wrong, not the mode.
  2. P2Color is meaning; category rides on shape. Five signals only: forward gold as line and fill, advisory text gold by day and lit gold by night, hazard ember, baseline slate, unmodeled indigo. On the action map a circle acts, a square measures, the ladder is the fitness course. Drug, lifestyle, and procedure are not hues; the arm lives in the ledger row. Gold on an instrument is the selection focus, and it counts as the surface's one gold.
  3. P3Hazard is a tile. Ember never runs as text on the night ground. Band words and status hazards sit in filled ember tiles with cream text at radius 4, in both registers, so the same markup is legal everywhere and true hazards outrank quiet advisories by construction.
  4. P4The instrument speaks voice. Values in the voice face, tabular, weight 500, with units, on charts and ledgers too. The audit face stays retired from the surface; it survives in the wordmark tag and in specification documents like this one.
  5. P5The scan clause. The one deliberate divergence from rule 08: group headers inside citation tables at density may run tracked caps, as waypoints for the scanning eye, not as prose. Anything read as a sentence stays sentence case. This clause is scoped to table group headers and nothing else.
  6. P6Furniture is tokened. Gridlines, leader lines, brackets, reference bands, and SVG marks draw from ink alphas and the gold wash through tokens, never hex, so the register flip carries the whole instrument, chart and all.
  7. P7The instrument confesses its limits. Axis captions state the value frame in place: actions plot net, diagnostics plot value if reclassified. AI-scored and unmodeled marks are indigo and dashed. Pending is declared, never blank. A chart that hides its frame is lying politely.
  8. P8Emphasis currency holds at density. One elevated card per screen. Data tables stay square specimens. Selection is the inset field. Under load, content yields to disclosure and collapse; air is never shrunk to fit more in.

03 · The reference surface

The chart, as proposed.

The reference surface is the working per-patient chart, not a mock: system-design/diagrams/aleron-actionmap-al47m-ds.html Six screens (patient data, risk models and the action map, vitality, care plan, journal, Aleron AI), two synthetic patients (AL-47M metabolic-risk, AL-56F low-vitality), both registers from one markup, zero console errors. It demonstrates every law above in place: the shape-not-hue action map with its evidence ledger and gate tethers, the ember hazard tiles, the tokened chart furniture surviving the register flip, the inset selection grammar, and the single-gold discipline with selection as the instrument's gold. On ratification, the design system cites this document in Section 06 the way it cites the Cabin, and physician chart work derives from this surface instead of re-deciding it.
04 · Open decisions

Four calls that ratification settles.

  1. O1The chart-label floor. A handful of dense SVG annotations sit at 9 to 10px, under the 10.5 functional floor. Either grant an instrument exception (chart annotations may run 9.5 minimum where they are secondary to a plotted mark) or enforce 10.5 and pay the density cost. Provisional call, 03 Jul: exception granted at 9.5; the reference surface is lifted to that floor for the field test.
  2. O2The three arms on the scatter. Drug, lifestyle, and procedure currently share the action circle; the arm distinction lives in the evidence ledger. Ratify the single circle, or assign a shape per arm and spend more of the map's shape budget on taxonomy. Provisional call, 03 Jul: the single circle stands; the shape budget stays spent on acts-versus-measures.
  3. O3The scan clause itself. P5 codifies tracked caps for citation-table group headers, the author's own recut of patient data. Confirm it as physician law, or return the headers to sentence case and let weight and air carry the grouping alone. Provisional call, 03 Jul: the clause holds as written, scoped to table group headers only.
  4. O4Rail scope. The inbox rail (Inbox, Panel, Reviews, Messages) and the chart rail (six sections) are different sets on the same anatomy. Confirm rails are fixed per surface family, so the chart's six are as law-bound as the member's six. Provisional call, 03 Jul: confirmed; rails are fixed per surface family.
