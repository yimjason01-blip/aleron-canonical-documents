The Cabin, tiered.

The member product register, decided from the register studies (five directions, then three spends of emphasis; Version C ratified). The premise it stands on: reading is the luxury. In a digital instrument, anything that slows the read is the opposite of premium. The environment should feel good, but ease of consuming the information comes first, and every law below serves it. Materials, wordmark, interaction grid, and the gold and hazard laws are inherited from the brand deck and the design system unchanged.

01 · The laws

Ten laws, all in service of the read.

  1. 01Reading is the luxury. Parse speed decides every contest between beauty and clarity. A member deciding about their body never pays a reading tax for a mood.
  2. 02One primary read per surface. Exactly one Tier 1 object answers "what do I look at first." On the plan, it is the physician's one thing.
  3. 03Emphasis is currency. Three tiers, each earned. Tier 1: the one elevated white card, one per surface. Tier 2: flat inset fields for data groups, enclosure without spotlight. Tier 3: naked on the paper, grouped by proximity alone. If everything is highlighted, nothing is.
  4. 04Proximity agrees with meaning. Space between groups is at least twice the space within them. Grouping is felt before it is read.
  5. 05Sentence case for everything anyone reads. Tracked caps are retired from product surfaces entirely, member and physician alike. The flight deck is dark, not harder to read.
  6. 06The value spine. Values sit right-aligned with units attached, forming one scannable column: the voice face, tabular figures, weight 500 against 400 prose. The audit face is retired from product surfaces; Space Mono remains the wordmark's tag alone. The instrument is the alignment, not the font.
  7. 07Hairlines separate rows, never groups. Rules live inside a Tier 2 field between rows. Groups are separated by tiers and space, not lines.
  8. 08Fillets, not bubbles, and the 10/12 line. Radius scale: 18 panel, 16 Tier 1, 12 Tier 2, 10 controls and nav, 4 signal tiles, circles only for avatars and dots. Radius declares role: at 10 and below it acts, at 12 and above it holds, and nothing crosses the line. Ink fills and strong outlines belong to controls alone; interactive text is ink at weight 500, annotations slate at 400. Containers nest concentric (18 holds 16 holds 12); a control keeps its radius wherever it sits. One shadow exists in the register: 0 12px 36px at 9% ink, reserved for Tier 1. Controls never float.
  9. 09Signals are inherited law. Gold is never a control and never a trim along a card edge; the gold fix is the bar in the label, never larger. Advisory is text gold on light grounds. Ember is hazard only, text on light grounds, a filled tile with cream text on dark. The interaction grid decides every pairing.
  10. 10Photography is a moment, not chrome. The destination layer opens arrivals, onboarding, the quarterly reveal, and never sits behind data.

04 · Scope and rollout

Where this register applies.

  1. 01This is the member day register. Every member-facing product surface adopts it: plan, your data, quality of life, risk, chat, appointments, onboarding.
  2. 02Dusk is the member evening companion. Warm graphite, indirect gold light, opt-in. It follows the same tier grammar when it is cut; it has not been cut yet.
  3. 03Symmetry remains the physician register. The flight deck keeps its dark layered grammar; instrument caps survive there and only there.
  4. 04First application: Your Data at full density. 104 markers is the stress test of laws 04, 06 and 07. Then the plan ships, then the rest.
  5. 05Lineage. Direction studies (five directions) to cabin studies (three spends of emphasis) to this register. The studies stay on disk as the record of the decision.
